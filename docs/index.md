## What is OPS?

Optical Pooled Screening (OPS) combines high-content imaging with pooled genetic screens to reveal what happens to cells across 40+ markers when any of 1,000 genes is knocked out—all without computational barriers.

### Why the OPS Explorer?

The OPS community faced three critical barriers to data accessibility:

1. **Scale and Complexity** — Multi-terabyte datasets require infrastructure beyond most labs.
2. **No Shared Standard** — Incompatible formats, metadata, and processing pipelines across labs meant data was effectively isolated.
3. **Analysis Expertise Barrier** — Users needed to replicate the entire computational stack of the generating lab just to engage with the data.

The OPS Explorer solves all three by centralizing standardized, harmonized datasets and providing intuitive interactive visualizations accessible to any researcher with a gene of interest.

## V1 Launch Data

At launch (June 2, 2026), the OPS Explorer hosts:

- **Biohub Dragonfly OPS experiments** — ~50–100 screens, 1,000 gene KOs, 40+ cellular markers
- **Matched CROP-seq data** — Paired transcriptomic profiles for the same perturbations
- **Vesuvius datasets** — Landmark data from the Broad/Cheeseman lab

All datasets are cross-validated, standardized, and ready for exploration and download.
